# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Splitism is an open, welcoming project. Everyone who participates — by filing issues, opening pull requests, commenting, or contributing in any other way — is expected to treat others with respect.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind and patient with other contributors, especially newcomers.
- Give and accept constructive feedback graciously.
- Focus criticism on ideas and code, not on people.
- Assume good intent; ask for clarification before assuming the worst.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks of any kind.
- Discriminatory language or jokes based on identity.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.
- Any conduct that would reasonably make others feel unwelcome.

## Enforcement

Violations can be reported by opening a private issue or emailing the maintainer directly. All reports will be reviewed and handled confidentially. Maintainers reserve the right to remove comments, close issues, or ban contributors who violate this policy.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other venue where project participants interact.
